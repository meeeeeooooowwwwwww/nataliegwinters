House Member’s Inquiry to Julie Kelly Signals Potential Shift in Trump’s Legal Battle

By KARI DONOVAN | 2025-03-13
Thursday marked a whirlwind of legal activity surrounding President Donald J. Trump, as he navigates a complex web of legal...

Thursday marked a whirlwind of legal activity surrounding President Donald J. Trump, as he navigates a complex web of legal challenges aimed at restricting his public engagement. Amidst the flurry of events, an intriguing update emerged from investigative journalist Julie Kelly, signaling a potential shift in the resistance against the public’s demands for a Criminal Conspiracy inquiry into Trump’s treatment.

“I did hear from one congressman’s office today, so that was encouraging they do want to see the information and the motions… so thank you Steve for putting pressure on there,” Kelly said to Steve Bannon.

[See WarRoom Video 13:00 min mark]

Kelly’s revelation came during Thursday evening WarRoom, hot on the heels of her report on the US Supreme Court’s demeanor during proceedings related to Trump’s prosecution.

Kelly noted that even Chief Justice Roberts, in particular, voiced concerns over the haste in reaching decisions by lower courts, suggesting a level of skepticism towards the ongoing legal proceedings against Trump.

Legal expert Mike Davis echoed these sentiments, foreseeing a narrow Supreme Court ruling in favor of presidential immunity from criminal prosecution for official acts. Davis outlined potential scenarios, indicating a significant impact on ongoing cases, including those led by Special Counsel Jack Smith and District Attorney Fani Willis. If the court favors Trump’s immunity, it could spell a historic victory for the former president and reshape the legal landscape surrounding his presidency.

Meanwhile, Bannon’s WarRoom provided a platform for experts and eyewitnesses to dissect the day’s legal events, ranging from the US Supreme Court hearing to the ‘Hush money’ trial in New York City and felony charges against Trump supporters. Sam Faddis appeared Thursday evening and emphasized the broader implications of what he termed “lawfare” against Trump, portraying it as a cautionary tale for supporters of the MAGA movement. Faddis and other guests underscored the perceived intent to silence dissenting voices and enforce a monolithic narrative.

For more on the day’s guests, see the show notes for AM and PM shows, for April 25.

However, amidst the legal skirmishes, a ray of hope emerged as Julie Kelly hinted at that breakthrough. According to Kelly, one courageous House member reached out to her, expressing interest in her investigation into redacted court documents related to classified documents in Trump’s trial. While the identity of this representative remains undisclosed, their inquiry signifies a growing interest in shedding light on the intricacies of Trump’s legal battles.

Julie Kelly And Mike Davis Discuss President Trump’s Supreme Court Immunity Case

Despite the optimism surrounding potential Supreme Court rulings, Bannon remains steadfast in his calls for further action against Republicans in DC. He advocates for the US House to initiate a Criminal Conspiracy inquiry against Democrat Joe Biden and others implicated in actions against Trump. For Bannon and his allies, the pursuit of justice extends beyond individual legal victories to a broader examination of alleged political machinations.

In conclusion, Thursday’s legal developments underscore the ongoing battle between Trump and his adversaries, characterized by intricate legal maneuvers and calls for accountability.
