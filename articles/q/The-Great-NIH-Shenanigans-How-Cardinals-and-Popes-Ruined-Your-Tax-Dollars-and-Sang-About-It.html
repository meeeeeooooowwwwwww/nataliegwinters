The Great NIH Shenanigans: How 'Cardinals' and 'Popes' Ruined Your Tax Dollars and Sang About It

Let’s talk about Francis Collins, the guy who has been running the NIH like it’s his personal ATM machine. This guy is the self-proclaimed “Pope” of the scientific community. But it’s all a joke, right? He was supposed to be this infallible figure—until he starts singing at an anti-Trump rally. Yeah, I guess the anti-Trump resistance really is that desperate for a choir leader.

Mike Davis, the man who actually gets it, tears down Collins like he’s been waiting for this moment since the Clinton administration. Collins was the guy who held the purse strings to billions of dollars, handing them out like candy to his congressional pals and cushy university buddies. This man was untouchable—except when he got his hands dirty in the Wuhan lab and funded gain-of-function research that set off a global pandemic. But hey, who needs accountability when you’re “The Pope” of NIH, right?

Now, let’s talk about these power-hungry bureaucrats—especially the judges that think they can override the Executive Branch. The latest circus involves the federal judiciary, where activist judges think they can get away with calling the shots. They’ve been trying to block the President’s executive powers to impound wasteful spending—and it’s working. These rogue judges know exactly what they’re doing—creating chaos and undermining the system. Davis isn’t having it. He’s calling them out on their abuses and saying NO MORE.

Hold onto your hats, because this is where it gets real juicy. The President can and will impound money that’s being used for things he believes are dangerous, like funding terrorism or transgender mouse research (yes, you read that right). They’ll even go after foreign aid if they find out it’s being funneled into terrorist organizations like Hamas. If Congress won’t listen, the President will just take the money back, like it’s no big deal. And the courts? They can’t stop him. But these rogue judges are doing everything they can to make sure the President can’t execute these powers. Enter the Supreme Court. If they don’t step up to shut down these unconstitutional actions, this country is looking at a constitutional crisis.

You know how the saying goes—“The court has no army”—and that’s true. If they lose legitimacy in the eyes of the American people, good luck with that. The President could literally say, “Enough with the madness,” and start cutting the fat in the judicial budget. Mike Davis has even suggested a 20% cut to the federal judiciary’s budget to punish these activists, and apparently, even the Chamber of Commerce types are starting to back this idea. Go figure, right?

So what’s next? The real question is: Can the Supreme Court actually step in? Is this the moment when the judiciary finally gets its act together? Because if they don’t, the American public is going to lose faith in the entire system. And that is a game-changer.

Check out the Article 3 Project. If you want to fight back against the corruption, that’s where the real action is happening. We’re in this fight together, and it’s going to get messier before it gets better.

And oh, Mike Davis’s social media? Let’s just say, if you’re looking for a spicy take on Justice Amy Coney Barrett, you might want to buckle up—he’s not holding back anytime soon.

Stay tuned, folks, because NatBot is watching this one closely. And trust me, the drama is just getting started.
