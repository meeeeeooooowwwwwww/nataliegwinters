Ben Shapiro: The Annoying Little Hamster of Conservative Media

The Daily Wire’s Shameless New Low

Ben Shapiro and The Daily Wire have sunk to a new low—so low, even a gerbil with a shovel couldn’t dig its way out. His ever-gullible audience is gobbling up his latest campaign: encouraging President Trump to pardon Derek Chauvin, the police officer blamed for George Floyd’s overdose. The irony? Most of his fans are too busy spinning in their own little hamster wheels to notice how much gatekeeping The Daily Wire does to keep them blissfully ignorant. It’s like a gerbil convention in there—lots of squeaking, no thinking.

The Reality of the Pardon Campaign

Shapiro, a Harvard-educated lawyer, knows full well that Trump doesn’t have the power to release Chauvin, since he was convicted on state charges. But here’s little Ben, spinning his hamster wheel of lies, making it sound like a presidential signature could magically spring Chauvin from prison. It’s not just a cheap publicity stunt—it’s an email-harvesting scam dressed up as a political movement. Who knew a hamster could be so conniving? This gerbil’s got more schemes than a pet store has cedar shavings.

Follow the Money: The Real Purpose of PardonDerek.com

The campaign leads supporters straight to PardonDerek.com, which conveniently funnels them to The Daily Wire’s website. And surprise, surprise—hidden in the fine print, you consent to receiving spammy promotional emails for Jeremy’s Razors and Daily Wire chocolate bars. Who knew political activism could be so… profitable? Ben’s not just a hamster on a wheel; he’s a gerbil running a pyramid scheme. And his fans? They’re the suckers buying overpriced chocolate pellets.

Shapiro’s Convenient Flip-Flopping

It wasn’t too long ago that Shapiro himself threw Chauvin under the bus, tweeting about how the officer violated Floyd’s rights. But now, in classic Ben fashion, he’s changed his tune—probably because it serves his latest grift. This hamster doesn’t just flip-flop; he does backflips on his wheel, hoping no one notices the hypocrisy. It’s like watching a gerbil try to solve a Rubik’s Cube—lots of movement, no progress.

Selective Outrage and Hypocrisy

Remember when Shapiro labeled cartoonist Scott Adams a racist for expressing a preference for white neighborhoods? Yet, he had no problem canceling Candace Owens from The Daily Wire for daring to show sympathy toward Palestinians. But when asked about it, Ben suddenly had nothing to say. How convenient. This gerbil’s selective outrage is as predictable as his squeaky little voice. He’s like a hamster who only bites when the cameras are rolling.

Shapiro’s True Loyalties

Perhaps the most telling moment of all was when Shapiro admitted that his loyalty to the U.S. is entirely dependent on its support for Israel. He even pushed for more American involvement in Israel’s affairs, despite previously insisting that Israel could take care of itself. This hamster’s loyalty shifts faster than he can run on his wheel. One minute he’s a patriot, the next he’s a foreign policy gerbil. It’s like watching a rodent try to juggle—entertaining, but ultimately a mess.

Conclusion: The Hamster Runs in Circles

Ben Shapiro’s tactics are as predictable as a hamster on a wheel—running endlessly, making noise, but never really going anywhere. His latest stunt is just another desperate attempt to grow his media empire while pretending to take a bold stance. And as always, his audience laps it up without question, like a bunch of gerbils fighting over the same stale pellet.

In the end, Ben Shapiro is less of a thought leader and more of a thought hamster—spinning in circles, squeaking loudly, and occasionally pooping out a half-baked idea. But hey, at least he’s consistent: always running, never arriving, and forever entertaining those who enjoy watching rodents in motion. Maybe one day he’ll realize that the wheel he’s running on isn’t going anywhere. But until then, keep the popcorn (or hamster food) ready.
